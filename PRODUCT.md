# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, served equally:

1. **Curious visitors**: people who found Skeinos or an Aakkagam game and clicked through to see who made it. They arrive with goodwill and low intent; the job is orientation ("what is this workshop, what else is here").
2. **Potential clients**: businesses or individuals evaluating Muthu for freelance/agency work once portfolio content lands. Their job is assessment: does the craft on this page prove the craft they'd be buying.

Both audiences visit briefly, on any device, and should leave in under a minute knowing what Aakkagam is and where to go next.

## Product Purpose

Landing page and hub for aakkagam.com (ஆக்கம், Tamil for "creation"). Routes visitors to the two subdomains, skeinos.aakkagam.com (local-first AI-chat organizer extension) and games.aakkagam.com (ancient board games in the browser), and will later exhibit portfolio work built for clients. Success: the page itself reads as evidence of care, and visitors reach the right destination without friction.

## Brand Personality

**Precise, minimal, warm.**

A small workshop for quiet software: free tools and games, no ads, no accounts, nothing sold. Editorial restraint; warmth comes from typography, language, and one culturally rooted mark (the kolam), not from ornament. The first-five-seconds feeling is calm craft: unhurried, handmade, confident without selling.

## Anti-references

- **SaaS landing template**: hero + feature grid + CTA buttons + gradient blobs + social proof strip.
- **Corporate agency site**: stock-photo polish, "we deliver solutions" tone, case-study carousels.
- **Ethnic kitsch**: temple borders, gold-on-maroon, Tamil motifs used as decorative wallpaper. The kolam is a drawn mark with meaning, not a theme.

## Design Principles

1. **The page is the portfolio.** Every detail (type, spacing, the kolam's line) is itself the proof of craft; no claims needed.
2. **One mark, deeply considered.** Cultural rootedness lives in a single meaningful element done well, never spread as decoration.
3. **Say less, mean it.** Copy is short, concrete, and free of marketing language; "no ads, no accounts, nothing sold" is the voice.
4. **Warmth through restraint.** Minimal structure, generous whitespace; warmth arrives via type choices, language, and material color, not effects.
5. **Route, don't retain.** The page succeeds when people leave it quickly for the right destination.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Basics as a floor: WCAG AA contrast, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on all animation (the kolam draw included), keyboard-visible focus, semantic HTML. Tamil text marked up with correct `lang` attributes.
